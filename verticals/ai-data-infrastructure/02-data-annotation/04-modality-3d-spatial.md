# 4. 3D / Spatial Annotation

## What Goes In

Point clouds, depth maps, mesh models, and spatial sensor data from LiDAR, radar, stereo cameras, and structured light sensors.

## What Comes Out

Labeled 3D structures where objects, surfaces, and free space have been identified and categorized in three dimensions. Essential for anything that moves through physical space.

## Annotation Types

### LiDAR Point Cloud Annotation
Labeling individual points or clusters in a 3D point cloud generated by laser scanning.
- **Example**: In an autonomous vehicle scene, label points belonging to cars, pedestrians, cyclists, traffic signs, and road surfaces.
- **Complexity**: Millions of points per scene; occlusions and varying point density make this difficult.

### 3D Bounding Boxes
Drawing rectangular prisms around objects in 3D space.
- **Example**: A car in a point cloud gets a box with length, width, height, and orientation in 3D coordinates.
- **Metrics**: 3D IoU (Intersection over Union) measures spatial overlap accuracy.

### Mesh Segmentation
Labeling regions on 3D mesh surfaces (used in robotics, medical imaging, and graphics).
- **Example**: In a 3D scan of a building, label walls, floors, windows, and doors as separate mesh regions.

### SLAM / Depth Annotation
Labeling simultaneous localization and mapping data and depth estimates.
- **Example**: Annotating loop closure points where a robot recognizes it has returned to a previous location.

## Open vs. Proprietary

Most 3D annotation is **proprietary** because the primary use case is autonomous vehicles, where companies guard their data fiercely. Open datasets (KITTI, nuScenes, Waymo Open) exist for research but are small compared to proprietary fleets.

## The Stalwart

**nuScenes**. Released by Motional (formerly Aptiv), it is one of the richest open autonomous driving datasets: 1,000 scenes with 1.4 million camera images, 390,000 LiDAR sweeps, and 23 object classes with full 3D bounding boxes and tracking. A large automotive company gave away data that would cost millions to collect, accelerating research for the entire field.
