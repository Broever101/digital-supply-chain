# 6. Scientific & Academic

## What Goes In

Rigorous, peer-reviewed, and experimentally validated data from researchers worldwide. Genomic sequences, protein structures, astronomical observations, and social science surveys.

## What Comes Out

Foundation datasets that train models to understand biology, chemistry, physics, and human behavior at a fundamental level. AlphaFold was trained on protein databases. GPT-4 was trained partly on arXiv papers.

## Key Players

| Name | URL | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| PubMed / PMC | https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov | Biomedical literature and abstracts |
| arXiv | https://arxiv.org | Preprint server for physics, math, CS papers |
| GenBank | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/genbank | Annotated DNA and RNA sequences |
| Protein Data Bank (PDB) | https://www.rcsb.org | 3D structures of proteins and nucleic acids |
| Hugging Face Datasets | https://huggingface.co/datasets | Community hub for publishing ML datasets |
| JSTOR / Semantic Scholar | https://www.semanticscholar.org | AI-powered academic search and analysis |
| Kaggle Datasets | https://www.kaggle.com/datasets | Community-contributed datasets for ML competitions |

## Open vs. Proprietary

**Mostly open**, but with caveats. PubMed is free. arXiv is free. GenBank and PDB are free. However, full-text access to paywalled journals (Elsevier, Springer) requires subscriptions, and publishers are increasingly selling "AI training licenses" on their archives.

## The Stalwart

**arXiv**. Started by Paul Ginsparg in 1991 as an email server for physics preprints. Now hosts 2+ million papers across every scientific discipline, completely free, with no paywall, no peer-review delay, and no publisher markup. It is the single most important text corpus for training scientific AI models, and it runs on a shoestring budget.
