# 12. Domain-Specific / Vertical Data

## What Goes In

Specialized data generated by niche industries: legal documents, real estate transactions, agricultural yields, energy consumption patterns, and logistics routes.

## What Comes Out

Training data for vertical AI models: contract review, property valuation, crop optimization, energy grid prediction, and supply chain forecasting.

## Open vs. Proprietary

Almost entirely **proprietary**. Vertical industries guard their data as a competitive advantage. The exceptions: some government agricultural data (USDA), court records (PACER), and property records (county assessors) are public but fragmented.

## The Stalwart

**RECAP / Free Law Project**. A non-profit that scrapes US federal court records from PACER — a government system that charges $0.10 per page — and makes them free. CourtListener, their search engine, hosts millions of legal documents that would cost researchers thousands of dollars to access through official channels. The law should be free to read, and they make it so.
