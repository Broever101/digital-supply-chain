# 7. Government / Public Records

## What Goes In

Data collected by governments as part of their normal operations: census forms, tax filings, patent applications, weather station readings, and environmental monitoring.

## What Comes Out

Authoritative, large-scale datasets that describe populations, economies, climates, and innovations. Often used as ground truth to validate commercial datasets.

## Key Players

| Name | URL | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| US Census Bureau | https://www.census.gov | Demographic and economic data |
| SEC EDGAR | https://www.sec.gov/edgar | Public company filings and financial disclosures |

| EU Open Data Portal | https://data.europa.eu | European Union public data |

| OpenSecrets | https://www.opensecrets.org | Political spending and lobbying data |

## Open vs. Proprietary

Almost entirely **open and free** by law in most democracies. The challenge is not access — it is fragmentation, format inconsistency, and the expertise required to clean and combine data from thousands of separate sources.

## The Stalwart

**OpenCorporates**. A small team built the largest open database of companies in the world — 200+ million companies across 140+ jurisdictions — by scraping and normalizing public registries. No government built this. No giant corporation owns it. Just a dedicated team and the belief that corporate transparency should not require a Bloomberg terminal.
