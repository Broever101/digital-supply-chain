# AI Data Infrastructure

## Why This Matters

Artificial intelligence is the buzzword, but data infrastructure is the oxygen. Before a chatbot can answer your question or a self-driving car can recognize a stop sign, someone has to decide what the internet looks like, label millions of images, and move terabytes of information across the world without a single byte getting lost.

This vertical maps how AI labs get their raw material — data — and turn it into something a machine can learn from.

## The Supply Chain at a Glance

Think of it like mining:
1. **Prospecting** — Finding where the data lives
2. **Mining** — Extracting raw data from the web, sensors, or documents
3. **Refining** — Cleaning, labeling, and quality-checking the data
4. **Shipping** — Moving the finished dataset to the training facility

Each step has its own tools, companies, and hidden gems.

## The Big Picture

The data annotation market alone — just one step in this chain — is projected to grow from roughly $5 billion today to over $29 billion by 2032. That is not because more people are clicking on pictures of traffic lights. It is because every industry now needs custom, high-quality training data, and the infrastructure to produce it at scale did not exist five years ago.
